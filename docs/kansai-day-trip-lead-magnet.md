## Route-Specific Practical Notes

Kyoto / Nara:
- Strong for first-time visitors.
- Can involve crowds, stairs, deer park walking, shrine paths, and limited rest.
- Food flexibility depends heavily on route timing.

Amanohashidate / Ine:
- Beautiful but long travel day.
- Weather, vehicle time, toilets, and lunch timing matter.
- Better for travelers who enjoy scenic drives and slower coastal stops.

Kobe / Himeji:
- Easier by train for many travelers.
- Private car only makes sense if combining scattered stops or traveling with
  group constraints.

Awaji / Wakayama / Lake Biwa:
- More spread out.
- Private car may solve route complexity, but costs and timing need checking.

## Operator Message Template

English:
Hello,

I am considering booking this Kansai day trip.

Could you please confirm:
- Where exactly is the meeting point?
- Are there toilet breaks during the route?
- Is lunch included, optional, or self-arranged?
- How much walking or stair use should we expect?
- Is this route suitable with a stroller or older traveler?
- Can we bring luggage, or should we avoid bringing bags?
- What happens if the weather is poor?

Thank you.
