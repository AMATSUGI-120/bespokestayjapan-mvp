# Lead Magnet and Product Boundary

Updated: 2026-07-11 JST

## Purpose

BSJ uses free planning samples to build trust and collect email subscribers.
Those samples should be genuinely useful, but they should not replace the value
of a paid kit.

The goal is not to make the free samples worse. The goal is to make the free and
paid offers do different jobs.

## Free Samples

Free samples should help travelers:

- Notice the practical problem.
- Understand what to confirm before relying on a hotel, restaurant, bath, train
  route, or booking-site label.
- See that BSJ understands Japan-specific friction points.
- Save a small, practical tool to their phone.
- Join the email list for future updates.

Free samples may include:

- Short checklists.
- A few phrase cards.
- Example inquiry wording.
- Simple comparison prompts.
- Explanations of tricky terms such as public bath, private bath, in-room bath,
  fish-based dashi, or luggage delivery.
- Clear disclaimers that policies and answers must be confirmed directly.

Free samples should avoid:

- Full hotel shortlists.
- Full Google Maps.
- Full Google Sheets.
- Extensive confirmed / needs-confirmation hotel notes.
- Complete regional comparison tables.
- A finished decision framework that removes the need for a paid product.
- Direct PDF download buttons on public pages when list-building is the current
  priority.

## Paid Kits

Paid kits should help travelers move from awareness to action.

Paid kits may include:

- Condition-specific hotel shortlists.
- Google Map links or saved-map style resources.
- Google Sheet comparison tables.
- Confirmation status and dates.
- Source URLs and notes.
- English inquiry templates.
- Booking-before-you-click checklists.
- Practical warnings and edge cases.
- Suggested next steps when a detail is unclear.

Paid kits should not promise:

- Hotel reservations.
- Availability.
- Tattoo acceptance, dietary safety, pet acceptance, luggage handling, or
  accessibility outcomes.
- That policies will remain unchanged.
- That BSJ can complete the accommodation booking for the traveler.

## First Paid Kit Candidate

Working title:
`Kansai Tattoo-Friendly Stay Kit`

Validation rule:
Do not build the full kit until BSJ has checked current metrics and sent a
beehiiv demand-check email.

Expected paid value:

- Kansai hotel shortlist.
- Bath-type explanation for tattooed travelers.
- Public bath / private bath / in-room bath distinction.
- Tattoo wording caveats.
- Google Map.
- Google Sheet comparison.
- Confirmed / needs-confirmation notes.
- Hotel inquiry templates.
- Pre-booking checklist.

Free sample role:
The free tattoo-related samples should explain what to ask and why it matters.
They should not include the complete shortlisted hotel database or full map.

## Metrics Before Build

Before building a paid kit, record:

- beehiiv subscriber count.
- beehiiv Email 1 delivery/open/click/reply status if available.
- PostHog clicks for lead magnet CTAs.
- PostHog clicks for product teaser CTAs.
- Travelpayouts revenue or click data if relevant.
- Any direct replies asking for tattoo, bath, hotel, food, or luggage help.

## Build Decision

Proceed to a small paid kit only if at least one of these signals appears:

- Several subscribers reply that they would buy or consider buying.
- Product teaser clicks are meaningfully higher than general guide clicks.
- Tattoo/bath pages receive repeat traffic or engaged sessions.
- Travelers ask concrete questions about hotels, baths, or confirmation status.

If signals are weak, keep collecting email subscribers and improve the free
education sequence before making a full product.
