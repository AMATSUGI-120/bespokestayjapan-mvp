# Experience Affiliate Research - BSJ

Updated: 2026-07-11 JST

## Purpose

BSJ can use activity and experience affiliate links without turning into a
generic tour list. The article should cover the main bookable experience, then
use BSJ's practical notes as the trust and email-signup hook.

Primary monetization now:
- Klook links where the category is active and commissionable.

Future monetization:
- GetYourGuide and Viator links after the BSJ project qualifies for approval
  with stable monthly traffic for at least three consecutive months.

Email-list role:
- Keep public articles useful.
- Do not hide safety, cancellation, age, or accessibility concerns.
- Put deeper practical notes behind email signup: meeting point checks, luggage
  suitability, toilet notes, food-need prompts, child/older-traveler checks,
  review-reading notes, and comparison sheets.

## Klook Program Notes

Observed in Travelpayouts for the BSJ project:
- Klook is available.
- Standard visible reward: 2-5%.
- Temporary visible campaign through Sep 30, 2026:
  - Tours & Sightseeing: 10%
  - Activities & Experiences: 10%
  - Hotels: 8%
- Excluded or sensitive categories include Food & Dining, Shopping Vouchers,
  eSIM top-ups, Events, and Flights. Food Coupon exception appears limited to
  Hong Kong & Macau users booking local food coupons there.

Practical rule:
- Prioritize tours, sightseeing, activities, transfers, airport transport,
  attraction tickets, workshops, ski/snow, and car charters.
- Treat food tours and restaurant bookings as content topics first, not primary
  Klook affiliate targets, unless commissionability is verified case by case.

## Article Structure

Use this structure for each article:

1. Lead with the travel problem.
2. Explain when the activity category is worth booking.
3. Show public practical checks.
4. Add 3-5 Klook examples or a category-level Klook CTA.
5. Offer a free checklist by email for deeper notes.
6. Mention confirmation support only when uncertainty affects booking decisions.
